Prehistory and Early Middle Ages in the Netherlands
--------------------------------------------------
• Hunter-gatherer communities lived in the region during the last Ice Age, adapting to tundra-like conditions.
• Around 4000 BC, the Funnelbeaker culture introduced agriculture to the lowlands.
• In the first century BC, Roman forces arrived; the southern area became part of the empire.
• The Rhine served as a northern frontier (limes), with forts and roads anchoring Roman presence.
• After Rome’s decline, Germanic groups—especially the Franks—consolidated power in the region.
• During the 9th–10th centuries, Viking raids targeted coastal settlements and river trade routes.
